Banana Cacao Protein Bars

A chewy, nutrient-dense bar that tastes like a brownie but eats like a meal. It includes the optional egg for structure, the protein powder for macros, and the superfood toppings for that final touch.

Overview

  • Yields: 8 Bars
  • Prep time: 10 mins
  • Bake time: 20 mins
  • Oven Temp: 350°F (175°C)

Ingredients

The Base:

  • 3 Medium ripe bananas (the more spots, the better)
  • 1.5 cups Old fashioned oats
  • 1⁄4 cup Unsweetened cacao powder
  • 2 scoops (~60g) Chocolate or vanilla protein powder
  • 1 Large egg
  • 1 tsp Baking powder
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 1⁄2 tsp Cinnamon
  • Pinch of Sea salt
  • (optional) 1 tbsp Hemp hearts

Instructions

  1. Prep the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line an 8x8 inch square baking pan with parchment paper, leaving a little overhang on the sides for easy removal.
  2. Mash & Mix Wet: In a large bowl, mash the bananas until mostly smooth. Whisk in the egg and vanilla extract.
  3. Add Dry Ingredients: Stir in the oats, cacao powder, protein powder, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. Mix until no dry pockets of powder remain. Note: If the batter is very stiff, add 1-2 tbsp of water or almond milk.
  4. Press into Pan: Transfer the batter to the prepared pan. Use a spatula to press it down firmly and spread it into the corners.
  5. Add optional toppings: Sprinkle the hemp hearts and a little extra flaky salt over the top.
  6. Bake: Bake for 18–22 minutes. The center should feel set and firm to the touch.
  7. Cool & Slice: This is the most important step! Let the bars cool completely in the pan. Lift them out using the parchment paper edges and slice into 8 even bars.

Nutrition Profile (Per Bar)

  • Calories: 220 kcal
  • Protein: 11g
  • Carbs: 28g
  • Fiber: 5g
  • Healthy Fats: 7g

Storage Tip: Keep these in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days or freeze them for a quick grab-and-go snack later.
